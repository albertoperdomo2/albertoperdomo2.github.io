About

Well, hello there! My name is Alberto Perdomo, but you can call me just Alberto. I'm a jack-of-all-trades with great passion for technology. With a Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Automation Engineering from [ULPGC](https://www.ulpgc.es/), and a Master's degree in Biomedical and Biomechanical Engineering from [Vilnius Tech](https://vilniustech.lt/), I'm basically a walking, talking, engineering machine. Throughout my academic and professional career, I've developed a strong passion for machine learning, systems, Linux, infrastructure and devops practices. Even though I've been working as a software engineer for a few years now, I'm not your average desk jockey. No, sir. I've been a research assistant at VGTU, DevOps engineer in various companies, and now I'm Infrastructure engineer in the Deep Learning team at Crisalix Labs, where I get to work with an amazing group of people. But don't let my chill vibes fool you, I'm a serious problem solver when it comes to engineering, particularly software. I love nothing more than tackling a difficult problem and figuring out the solution. It's not all about work for me and when I'm not tinkering away at some new project, I'm busy traveling around, hitting the gym or doing a few laps in the pool. When it comes to research, my focus has been on hyperspectral imaging (HSI) and CV systems for telerehabilitation. I've got a particular interest in the intersection of technology and healthcare, and I'm always on the lookout for new opportunities to make a positive impact on the world. Now, you might be thinking, "wow, this guy sounds like a real workhorse." And you're not wrong! I'm highly motivated, analytical, and goal-driven. However, I'm not all work and no play. I'm a team player at heart, and I love collaborating with others to achieve a common goal. Plus, I've got some pretty sweet communication and leadership skills to boot that I have been lucky enough to hone over the years. So, if you're looking for a multi-talented, hard-working, and all-around awesome engineer, look no further! Please, keep in mind that at this moment in time **I'm not looking for full-time roles** but I'm always up for a new challenge and excited to continue growing and learning in my career. P.S. This site ain't no fancy schmancy flashy thing. Keep in mind that it's based on plain HTML+CSS, so don't go expecting any bells and whistles. But hey, the real gem here is the content. So stick around and give it a read, yeah?
